In a collaboration seeded by 4Culture, artist Gage Hamilton is working with the SODO BIA and Urban Artworks to create The SODO Track.

What we're building

The SODO Track will transform 2-miles of building backs into a vibrant street art corridor for 43,000 bus and light rail riders to experience as they enter Downtown Seattle each day.

Learn about us

Who is painting the walls?

Local, national, and international artists will be commissioned to create an imaginative raceway of art in motion.

What our schedule looks like

32 buildings will be painted by 54 artists over consecutive summers starting in 2016. Phase 1 will transform the northeast corner of the corridor--from Royal Brougham Way to Lander Street. Phase 2 will paint the southeast corner, and Phase 3 will complete the west side of the Track.

For many years, Seattleâ€™s community of artists, curators, and cultural advocates imagined turning the SODO busway into a vibrant street art corridor. Now, we are enacting the vision. This year 4Culture launched The SODO Track, hiring artist Gage Hamilton to develop a plan to transform two miles of building backs into an international arts destination with large scale murals. Partners 4Culture, Sound Transit, and the SODO Business Improvement Association (BIA) provided the seed money to get the project started, and Urban Artworks pledged their help to prepare the walls. Now additional funds are needed to launch the next phase of the project that will engage 50 international, national, and local artists to paint 32 properties over three summers.

Sponsorship

Your donation will help pay for project-related expenses such as artist honorariums, paint, equipment rentals, and artist travel and lodging.
